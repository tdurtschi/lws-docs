Morning Dew
Upon each blade of grass so green, Diamonds catch the early light, Nature's jewels, fresh and keen, Sparkling in the morning bright. Each droplet holds the rising sun, A prism dancing in the breeze, Until the day has just begun, And melts away among the trees.

The Ancient Oak
Standing tall through centuries gone, Witness to both joy and strife, Its branches reaching toward the dawn, A testament to timeless life. In its shade the children play, Birds make homes within its bark, Through storm and drought it finds a way, A monarch of the woodland park.
